Pre-Layout Review Checklist

This handy checklist ensures your schematics are all set and ready for the layout phase. Using this checklist is simple and easy to understand.

— Dario

Just go through the items listed below and mark them as done when you've completed each step.

I've noticed that as I gained more experience, I sometimes overlooked the basics while focusing on the more critical aspects. This checklist is designed to help you avoid such common mistakes.

As you become more experienced, you'll probably develop your unique style and create a personalized checklist.

Now, let's begin the review process.

Project Identification and Overview 0/4 Items
My Overview Page has a clear title that illustrates the name of the project
i Why this matters
Context
A clear project title prevents confusion during reviews and helps everyone immediately understand what the project is about.
I have added a revision number to identify the project version I am working with
i Why this matters
Context
Without proper version tracking, you risk manufacturing the wrong version or losing track of critical design changes.
Other Project details are updated, including the Title, revision, and version number, file name
i Why this matters
Context
Complete metadata ensures everyone works with the correct file and prevents costly version control mistakes.
My contact details are displayed on each page
✓Name and surname of the designer
✓Email address
✓Logo / Company name
i Why this matters
Context
Contact information enables quick resolution of questions during layout, manufacturing, and debugging.
Schematic Organization and Clarity 0/6 Items
I confirm that I have included a block diagram of the connections between components and systems
i Why this matters
Context
A system-level view helps identify signal flow, power distribution architecture, and potential issues before detailed work begins.
I confirm the title is displayed on each page and is descriptive of the functional block
i Why this matters
Context
Descriptive page titles enable quick navigation and make reviews more efficient for both you and your team.
The pages of the schematics are numbered for easy block location
i Why this matters
Context
Page numbers are essential for cross-referencing and discussions during design reviews.
Each block has an intuitive name and a page number for easy identification
i Why this matters
Context
Self-explanatory block names reduce confusion and speed up troubleshooting when issues arise.
The status of the schematic is clearly reported
i Why this matters
Context
Clear status marking prevents accidentally using outdated versions in production.
The date of the last update is clearly visible on each page
i Why this matters
Context
Timestamps help track design evolution and ensure you're working with the latest revision.
Design Details and Annotations 0/4 Items
I confirm that each module of my schematic is subdivided into clear blocks
i Why this matters
Context
Logical organization improves readability and makes it easier to isolate problems during debugging.
I confirm that each net has a net label assigned for easy identification
i Why this matters
Context
Descriptive net names prevent layout errors and make debugging infinitely easier than auto-generated names like 'N$42'.
I confirm that each symbol has consistent directionality to avoid confusion
i Why this matters
Context
Maintaining left-to-right signal flow reduces cognitive load and prevents connection mistakes.
I confirm that sensitive traces are labeled for attention during the layout phase
i Why this matters
Context
Marking critical signals alerts the layout engineer to apply special routing rules and constraints.
Protection and Debugging Features 0/14 Items
I confirm that each input and output connector has appropriate protections and/or filters
i Why this matters
Context
Every I/O needs protection against ESD, overvoltage, and transients. This is essential for product reliability.
I confirm that I added testing points and other tools for easy debugging and bring-up where necessary
i Why this matters
Context
Test points enable efficient bring-up, debugging, and field troubleshooting. Add them to all critical signals.
I confirm that each IC has bypass capacitors
i Why this matters
Context
Local decoupling is fundamental to power integrity and EMC compliance. Every IC needs bypass caps close to power pins.
I confirm that all unused pins are terminated
i Why this matters
Context
Floating inputs can cause oscillation, excessive power consumption, and EMI problems.
I confirm that polarized components are placed correctly according to their polarity
i Why this matters
Context
Reversed polarity causes immediate failure or damage. Always double-check orientation.
I confirm that the design includes necessary safety features such as overvoltage, overcurrent, and thermal protection
i Why this matters
Context
Protection against overvoltage, overcurrent, and thermal events prevents catastrophic failures and safety hazards.
I confirm that the design adheres to relevant industry standards and regulatory requirements (such as IEC standards for electronic equipment)
i Why this matters
Context
Compliance verification during design prevents expensive re-spins discovered during certification testing.
I confirm that all necessary power decoupling and filtering strategies are incorporated in the design
i Why this matters
Context
Document your decoupling and filtering strategy clearly so the layout engineer implements it correctly.
I confirm that user interface elements, if any (like LEDs, switches, and display interfaces), have been correctly implemented and documented
i Why this matters
Context
UI components need proper current limiting, debouncing, and signal levels. Document the requirements clearly.
I confirm that ESD protection measures are in place for all sensitive components and interfaces
i Why this matters
Context
Verify this item carefully during your schematic review.
I confirm that RF components and circuits (if any) have been reviewed for frequency, impedance matching, and interference issues
i Why this matters
Context
Verify this item carefully during your schematic review.
I confirm that provisions for calibration, if required, are incorporated into the design
i Why this matters
Context
If calibration is needed, plan for it now. Adding test points and access later is difficult or impossible.
I confirm that each subsystem (e.g., power supply, microcontroller unit, communication interfaces) has been individually verified for its functionality
i Why this matters
Context
Verify each major functional block independently before integration to catch issues early.
I confirm that any identified issues from previous design reviews have been addressed
i Why this matters
Context
Address all feedback from earlier reviews. Unresolved issues have a way of coming back.
Power and Signal Integrity 0/7 Items
I confirm that each power net has its voltage value and polarity clearly indicated
i Why this matters
Context
Clearly labeled power nets (+3V3, +5V, GND) prevent catastrophic wiring errors during layout.
I confirm that signal traces with high harmonic content have a series Zero-ohm resistor for prompt replacement if necessary
i Why this matters
Context
Verify this item carefully during your schematic review.
I confirm that I added terminations where necessary for impedance matching
i Why this matters
Context
Proper termination prevents reflections and ringing on high-speed signals. Document the termination strategy.
I confirm that power components have the correct power ratings
i Why this matters
Context
Component derating (typically 50-100%) ensures reliability under worst-case conditions.
I confirm that there are no crossing nets or wires on the schematics
i Why this matters
Context
Wire crossings create ambiguity and connection errors. Use net labels and proper connections instead.
I confirm that all external interfaces (connectors, ports) have been correctly defined and meet the requirements for signal, power, and mechanical robustness
i Why this matters
Context
Verify all connector pinouts, signal levels, and mechanical specifications against interface standards.
I confirm that inter-board or intra-board communication protocols and connections are correctly implemented
i Why this matters
Context
Verify proper implementation including pull-ups, terminations, and signal level compatibility.
Component and Connection Verification 0/8 Items
I confirm that all components used in the design are readily available or have identified substitutes to avoid supply chain issues
i Why this matters
Context
Component availability issues can delay production. Identify alternates for critical parts now.
I confirm that all components used in the design are sourced from reliable suppliers and are not at risk of becoming obsolete in the near future
i Why this matters
Context
Check manufacturer lifecycle status. Avoid parts marked NRND (Not Recommended for New Designs).
I confirm that I double-checked each pin of major components for correct connections
i Why this matters
Context
Manual verification of critical IC connections catches pin-swap errors before they become expensive.
I confirm that RX and TX pins are connected to the correct pins
i Why this matters
Context
Verify this item carefully during your schematic review.
I confirm that I added net ties for tracks with the same voltages but different roles
i Why this matters
Context
Net ties (0Ω resistors or ferrites) separate domains with the same voltage but different noise characteristics.
I confirm that each block is interconnected to the right block on the block diagram
i Why this matters
Context
A system-level view helps identify signal flow, power distribution architecture, and potential issues before detailed work begins.
I confirm that the design has been checked for interoperability with other systems or modules it will interface with
i Why this matters
Context
Confirm signal levels, timing, protocols, and connector compatibility with mating systems.
I confirm that key components such as microcontrollers, memory, and power regulators have been correctly implemented as per their datasheets
i Why this matters
Context
Read the complete datasheet and errata documents. Many design errors stem from incomplete datasheet review.
Layout and Mechanical Considerations 0/4 Items
I confirm that the input and output ports are designed according to the left-to-right convention
i Why this matters
Context
Following standard conventions significantly improves schematic readability and reduces errors.
I confirm that I added extra footprint between mounting holes and board return Ground

Context
Copper keepout around mounting holes prevents unintended chassis ground connections.
I confirm that I added fiducials and mounting holes to the mechanical page
i Why this matters
Context
Copper keepout around mounting holes prevents unintended chassis ground connections.
I confirm that a component represents the PCB in the Bill of Material
i Why this matters
Context
Verify this item carefully during your schematic review.
Compliance and Documentation 0/6 Items
I confirm that I added symbols for labels like CE mark, ESD, Hot surface, High-Voltage, UL certification, RoHS compliance etc.
i Why this matters
Context
Regulatory markings (CE, FCC, RoHS) should be schematic symbols so they're not forgotten during layout.
I confirm that differential nets are labeled and annotated accordingly
i Why this matters
Context
Differential pairs must be clearly labeled with consistent naming to ensure proper routing.
I confirm that the schematic looks clean and orderly
i Why this matters
Context
A clean, well-organized schematic reflects attention to detail and professionalism.
I have confirmed that I studied the datasheet of each IC and checked the errata files
i Why this matters
Context
Read the complete datasheet and errata documents. Many design errors stem from incomplete datasheet review.
I confirm that all critical signal paths (like clock lines, high-speed data) are optimized for minimal interference and delay
i Why this matters
Context
High-speed signals need direct routing with minimal vias and stubs. Document constraints in the schematic.
I confirm that a basic FMEA has been performed to identify potential failure modes and their impact on the circuit's performance
i Why this matters
Context
Verify this item carefully during your schematic review.
